The Unchecked AI Problem

The number in your AI report might be wrong. Right now, you have no way to know.

Most AI in production has nobody checking its work. It produces confident, fluent, plausible-sounding output — and the wrong numbers look exactly like the right ones.

CASDAM is a working production system that catches the errors before they reach your report.

The Bad Day

You have probably already lived one of these.

Three failure scenarios that happen every week somewhere in production. They share one root cause: nobody checked the AI's work before it reached someone who acted on it.

Scenario 1

Your weekly board update cited a customer churn rate. Two days later, the data team can't reproduce the number. The board doesn't know yet.

Scenario 2

Your AI dashboard summarized last quarter as "strong fulfillment performance." Your actual on-time rate was below SLA. The summary got there because the AI averaged in three weeks of stale data.

Scenario 3

Your CFO asked, "How do you know that?" about a number from your AI report. You couldn't answer.

None of these are model failures. They are governance failures — the AI did exactly what AI does, and there was no system in place to catch it.

Why It Happens

AI doesn't know what it doesn't know.

There is no internal alarm.

AI generates output by predicting plausible-sounding text based on patterns. It can't tell when it's generating something wrong — because being wrong looks identical to being right from inside its own process. There is no flag. There is no warning. The output just goes out.

You find out it was wrong when someone outside the system catches it. Sometimes in a meeting. Sometimes in a quarterly report. Sometimes never.

The Cure

What "checked AI" looks like.

Three structural mechanisms, layered. Each one is dumb on its own. Together they make wrong numbers nearly impossible to reach the report.

Step 1
An AI generates a claim and cites where it came from
↓
Step 2
A second, independent AI checks: does the source actually support this claim?
↓ PASS
claim continues
↓ FAIL
claim removed entirely
↓
Step 3
Numbers are independently recomputed by code (not AI). Code wins on any disagreement.
↓
Result
Only verified, source-cited claims reach your report.

Proof

This is a working production system. Not theory.

CASDAM is a six-stage pipeline that does exactly what's described above. It uses six different AI systems from six different companies — so the AI checking the AI shares no training data and no architecture with the AI being checked. Every number in the final report is independently recomputed in plain code. Every claim cites the source it came from. When the AI is wrong, you see the disagreement in the verification footer — not in the executive summary.
